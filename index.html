LCD & THE CHALLENGE

Leonard Cheshire Disability (LCD) is one of the UKâ€™s oldest and largest disability focused civil society organizations with over 15 years of experience in delivering inclusive education, employment and livelihoods, youth leadership programs and research across Africa, Asia and the UK. One of LCDâ€™s key initiatives is the Access to Livelihoods (A2L) program to help people with disabilities getting a decent job.


Their priority challenge is that the current information retrieval process related to identification, registration and assessment for the A2L program is resulting in beneficiary drop-out, and is neither providing an engaging experience to persons with disabilities, nor an effective process for LCD. While being relevant globally, the initial focus is on the A2L program in India.

MVP STATEMENT

Persons with disabilities can access and easily complete the registration by themself with guidance from a virtual assistant so that they can be confident and happy that they have taken the first step towards getting a decent job.

SOLUTION

We built a virtual registration assistant, Nandini, for Leonard Cheshire Disability (LCD) to allow job seekers with varying disabilities to digitally register themselves in an easy, engaging and accessible manner. Nandini guides the job seeker through 6 sections of questions to complete the initial registration process as the first step towards getting a decent job. Throughout the process Nandini provides multiple choice answers, picks up help requests, analyzes the user's emotional state, provides examples and reasoning behind her questions while always showing the progress and making sure the user is engaged.

The expected result is happier and more engaged users, fewer drop-outs during and post registration, freeing up LCD staff time, increased data quality and the primary goal; getting more people into relevant jobs.
Under the hood Nandini uses the IBM Bluemix Platform along with the IBM Watson Conversation, Tone Analyzer and Text-to-Speech services.

PROTOTYPE TIMELINE

FEBRUARY 2017

DESIGN THINKING WORKSHOPS 01/02

Initial creative workshop in London, UK, to ideate on concept and define scope for the prototype. Attendees from LCD UK and India as well as Accenture Corporate Citizenship and ACIT Cloud Studio.

09/

FIRST ITERATION WIREFRAMES

Defined concepts after the initial workshop, creating and iterating paper & digital wireframes. Focus on including key components for questions, progress and gamification in an accessible and engaging manner

21/

USER TESTING

On-site usability testing, with person with low visions at Accenture India offices, to evaluate and get input on the application. Design & UX was then refined based on observed user behavior and feedback.

FIRST DEVELOPMENT ITERATION DONE /16

Completed wireframes after several iterations with LCD and Accenture stakeholders. Based on this the first working version of the virtual registration assistant based on IBM Watson technology was implemented and ready for user testing.

23/

GRAPHICAL DESIGN COMPLETED

The graphical design was completed enabling the front-end team to radically improve the feel and accessibility of the application.

MARCH 2017

FINALIZING PROTOTYPE /02

Refining design and UX based on 2nd round of usability testing. Implemented additional intelligent features to better guide the user through the registration process. Showcasing the benefits and capabilities of the application through a live demo. Discussing next steps to move towards production deployment.

TESTIMONIALS

The ACIT methodology helped us review our current processes and identify areas for improvement from day one. In each sprint we were able to move very fast, and in only four weeks we managed to reduce the time spent on registration and assessment by more than half, while also making the process more engaging for our users.

When fully implemented, we expect the solution to significantly scale our reach and support further custom tailored services to be offered.
